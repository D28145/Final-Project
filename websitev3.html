Major Communication Technologies Post-1877

This website explores the major communication technologies invented after 1877, highlighting their relevance and impact on society. From the telephone to cell phones, each technological advancement has revolutionized the way we communicate, shaping the modern world.

Communicative Technology has been pivotal in shaping the history of the United States. It has enabled instant and widespread communication thereby fueling social and economic progress and laying the groundwork for subsequent technological breakthroughs that have collectively revolutionized society.

Communicative technology has played a critical role in United States history. Made by Alexander Graham Bell in 1876, the telephone has changed how people communicate over long distances. It replaced slower ways like letters and telegraphs. This made it easier for people and businesses to stay in touch. It helped businesses grow by making coordination and customer service better. Not but a few years later, the radio was invented in the early 20th century allowing for mass communication to broadcast news, music, and sports. Radios were the first means of communication that could reach millions of United States citizens with news, entertainment, and emergency info, bringing the nation together especially during times like World War II.

Invented by Philo Farnsworth in 1927, the television changed communication forever by bringing the visual side of communication to life, allowing homes to own their piece of the theater in their living room. It affected culture, politics, and social norms. It became a major way for advertising and public messages. The internet, which started from ARPANET in the 1960s to the World Wide Web in the 1990s, completely changed how people could communicate and access data globally, making communication and data accessible almost instantaneously. It impacted industries, education, and social interactions a lot. Finally, we have the mobile phone beginning with Martin Cooper's invention in 1973 made communication even easier allowing for communication on the go anywhere at anytime. Modern smartphones combined internet access, GPS, and applications to make a necessary tool for ease of life. The advancements of these technologies have paved United States History helping social and economic growth leading to the America we have today.

The Telephone (1876)

Alexander Graham Bells invention in 1876 revolutionized personal and business communication. Before the telephone, there was a reliance on slower methods of communication like letters and telegraphs. The revolutionary invention of the telephone allowed for us to instantly communicate over long distances, this completely changed how people and businesses communicated and interacted.1 This technological breakthrough helped businesses by allowing for the growth of customer service and coordination amongst its staff across long distances. The telephone overall laid the foundation for the future of global telecommunications networks, this influenced the subsequent communicative technologies that we will visit later on.2

The telephone did not only allow for incredibly fast communication but it also transformed the economic and social environment of the times. It allowed for the growth of suburbs enabling people to live farther from their place of work. this in turn influenced urban planning.3 The creation of the telephone also allowed for emergency services of the time to be more effective, greatly increase response times and saving more lives. 4

The telephone industry itself also became an incredibly massive sector for the United States, creating jobs and increasing technological advancements that led to the creation of other communicative devices.5 The transition from operator-based systems to automatic switching systems revolutionized the efficiency and reliability of the telephone.6 Overall the telephones impact of everyday life was like not other, and it changed how people interacted professionally and socially forever.7

Radio (Early 20th Century)

Radio technology, was developed in the beginning of the 20th century, brought a brand new era for mass communication. IT allowed for the transmission of voice and music to wide audiences, this transformed entertainment, emergency communication, and news. Radios were the first form of communication that allowed for the ability to reach millions of United States citizens, bringing the nation overall together, especially during the rough times of this era like World War II.1 Radio ended up playing a crucial role during the war providing a form of communication for real-time updates and increasing the overall morale. This invention laid out the future for the invention of the television and later the internet.2

The impact the radio had on culture and society was a major shift and change. It allowed for equal access to information, allowing people from all different backgrounds to receive the same news and information at the same time.3 Radio also played a major role in the popularity and rise of pop music, this helped ton create shared cultural experiences in the United States.4

Furthermore, the invention of the radio was critical in the development of advertising. It provided platforms for businesses to reach out to a massive amount of consumers, leading to the future rise of consumer culture in America.5 Radio advertisings success paved the pathway for television commercials and later on digital ads.6 The radio also churned the future innovations that would play critical roles in the future of communicative technologies.7

Television (1927)

The invention of television by Philo Farnsworth in 1927 changed the entertainment and information landscape forever. Television brought visual communication into homes, influencing the ability to relay culture, politics, and social norms.1 Television soon became the major platform for advertising and public messaging, this shaped the public opinion and pop culture in uncalculated ways. Television allowed for a new way for people to experience, news, sports, and entertainment allowing for shared experiences across all citizens in the Untied States.2

It quickly became the primary source for all information and entertainment uniting social interactions.3 Television also quickly became a battleground for political communication, allowing for politics to reach out directly to voters through advertisements.4

Television also allowed for the growth of the entertainment industry, creating numerous jobs for broadcasting and advertising. 5 The rise of television networks and cable TV allowed for a variety of available content, allowing for a diverse amount of audience members.6 Future technological advancements stemming from the invention of the television would pave the way for high definition tv and digital broadcasting.7

The Internet (1960s - 1990s)

The development of the internet starting with APARNET in the 1960's then to what we know as the World Wide Web today revolutionized global communication. The internet facilitated instant access to information, digital communication, and e-commerce.1 It now has become a part of everyday life, transforming businesses, education, and social life. The internet even today continues to evolve with advancements we can see like AI and the cloud, making it one of the most critical inventions ever made.2

The internet's change on business and commerce has been a pillar of the past few decades. It has created the rise of e-commerce, allowing consumers and businesses to shop and sell from anywhere at anytime.3 The internet has also facilitated working remote and telecommunication, changing workplace dynamics indefinitely.4

The internet has changed more than just businesses, it has also changed education allowing instant access to vast amounts of information and online resources. The internet has made education accessible to everyone, especially those stuck in remote areas.5 Social interactions have also been completely revolutionized by the internet, setting the landscape for social media platforms allowing users to meet and interact across the globe.6 The creation of the internet has been a pivotal role for the growth of other communicative technology, revolutionizing the world, and allowing for the future creation of the mobile phone and the laptop.7

Cell Phones (1973 - Present)

The invention of the cell phone made by Martin Cooper in 1973 started the beginning of mobile communication. Cell phone have started from bulky devices and have evolved to powerful smartphones that we can hold in our hands, including various tech like cameras, GPS, and internet connectivity. 1 Cell phones have also transformed how we can communicated, access information, and manage our day to day tasks making them one of the most important tools in our everyday life. Cell phone advancements have paved the way for the future of digital communication, and the phones that we have today are just a remnant of the once incredibly innovative creation called the mobile phone, that now allow us to connect from anywhere at anytime in the world.2

Cell phones have significantly impacted social interactions, allowing us to stay connected at incredibly long distances. The creation of smartphones has also revolutionized how we access and find information, providing us with things like apps and social media.3

The economic impact of cell phones is also very important to take note. The mobile phone industry created millions of jobs and generated new revenue streams worldwide.4Innovations in mobile phone technology has advanced other sectors such as health, education, and finance.5 Additionally, cell phones have become the most crucial tool in the world of emergency response and personal safety.6
